2   Spring Storm

This Circle of Life is all about Spring—Fecund Spring.  Philip Yenawine, the co-founder of Visual Thinking Strategies, a non-profit educational initiative, introduced me to this powerful, evocative word through his superb facilitation skills.  The etymology of the word fecund harkens back to the Latin word, “fruitful.” The word speaks of new life, palpable growth, the pregnant earth waiting to deliver spring.  This circle has the tingling energy of lightning, the swirl of spring winds, and rain charging the plants to grow. The beech nut in the center has split open to release a new tree.  Those early harbingers of spring--tulips--wend their way through the circle. Small fragile leaves burst from their buds. Sprouts emerge from the soil. I hope you feel the sense of movement and joy I have tried to convey.

On another level, this piece represents resurrection and new life.  As my granddaughter and I were walking through the woods in early spring we kept crunching on the acorns and beech nuts on the forest floor. I brought one of the split-open beechnuts home to draw for this Circle of Life and thought about Jesus’ parable of the seed in John 12.  Jesus was trying to prepare his disciples for his death and he told them, “The time has come for the Son of Man to be glorified. I tell you truly that unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains a single grain of wheat; but if it does, it brings a good harvest.”  Jesus was talking about his time to die and his glorious resurrection—the incredibly “good harvest.”  The wind and the crackling lightning in this mandala also speak of the incredible power of that resurrection and the power of God working in our hearts to bring us to Himself--God’s creation teaching us lessons about the Creator.
